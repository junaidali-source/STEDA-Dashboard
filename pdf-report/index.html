Summary
Findings & Recommendations
March 18, 2026
1
Strong Activation Rate
67.8% of enrolled teachers (915 of 1,349) activated their accounts within the first 9 days — well above the 50% benchmark for digital onboarding programmes.
✓ Target: Reach 80% by April 15
2
Lesson Plan Feature Traction
395 teachers generated 975 lesson plans with a 98.2% completion rate. Average of 2.47 plans per user signals genuine recurring engagement, not one-time exploration.
→ Promote to remaining 520 activated teachers
3
434 Teachers Unreached
32.2% of the enrolled cohort has not yet activated. District-level follow-up via school coordinators is recommended — particularly for districts with below-average rates.
! Targeted outreach campaigns needed
4
Active Community Engagement
551 members joined the WhatsApp community with 54 self-confirming registration in chat. Positive sentiment from ~20 teachers confirms grassroots satisfaction and peer advocacy potential.
→ Activate peer champion programme
5
Gender Gap to Address
Female teachers represent only 26% of the enrolled cohort. Inclusive recruitment strategies and female-focused onboarding sessions should be prioritised for the next cohort intake.
→ Target 35% female in next intake
6
Feature Expansion Opportunity
Coaching and other features show minimal usage compared to Lesson Plans. A structured feature introduction programme — including short tutorial videos — could unlock broader platform adoption.
→ Launch feature tutorial series in April
Overall Assessment
The STEDA onboarding phase has been a measurable success. With nearly 68% activation in under two weeks, strong lesson plan adoption, and genuine community enthusiasm, the programme is well-positioned to achieve full cohort engagement by end of April 2026.
A–
Programme Score
